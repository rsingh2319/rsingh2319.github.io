Background🐉

Tennis is a racket sport that is played either individually against a single opponent (singles) or between two teams of two players each (doubles). Each player uses a tennis racket that is strung with cord to strike a hollow rubber ball covered with felt over or around a net and into the opponent's court. The object of the game is to manoeuvre the ball in such a way that the opponent is not able to play a valid return. The player who is unable to return the ball validly will not gain a point, while the opposite player will.

Rules🙉

The rules of modern tennis have changed little since the 1890s. The Four Grand Slams🏆🏆

The four Grand Slam tournaments (also referred to as the Majors) are especially popular: the Australian Open played on hard courts, the French Open played on red clay courts, Wimbledon played on grass courts, and the US Open also played on hard courts.

Why you should play today <3

Tennis is a sport that contributes to our health and happiness long term. It can be played at any age and is a sport that stays with you for life. It keeps you active not only physically but also mentally. Individual sports are hard but they also allow for tremendous amounts of growth. People who play tennis can use skills like discipline, drive, and calmness not only on the court to further their tennis careers but off of it too to further other aspirations.
